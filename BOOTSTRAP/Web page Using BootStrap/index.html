Struggles of Auto Driver

Auto drivers wake up early in the morning to start their day. They drive all day under the hot sun or heavy rain. Traffic jams make their work slow and tiring. Sometimes they don’t get enough passengers and earn very little. Fuel and repair costs take away a big part of their money. They face rude or troublesome passengers sometimes. Long hours make them feel very tired and sick. They worry about accidents and their safety every day. Even though they work hard, many people do not respect them. Still, they keep driving, hoping to feed their families.

Struggles of Street Vendor

Street vendors wake up early to prepare their goods. They work under the scorching sun and heavy rain every day. Competition is tough and not everyone buys from them. They often carry heavy loads and stand for hours. Sometimes they face harassment from authorities or crowds. Their earnings are unstable and unpredictable. Long working hours make them physically exhausted. Despite hardships, they keep selling to feed their families. They rarely get recognition for their hard work. Their spirit keeps Chennai lively and colorful.

Struggles of IT Professionals

IT professionals start their day early and often work late into the night. They face tight deadlines and constant pressure to perform. Problem-solving and coding errors can be mentally exhausting. Meetings and client demands add to their stress. Long screen time affects their health and eyes. Work-life balance is hard to maintain. They constantly need to upgrade their skills to stay relevant. Despite challenges, they keep innovating and contributing to the city’s growth. Recognition is limited, but their work impacts millions. Their dedication drives Chennai’s digital world.

Struggles of Construction Workers

Construction workers start early in the morning, carrying heavy loads. They work under extreme heat, rain, and dust daily. The job is physically demanding and exhausting. Safety risks are high, and accidents are common. Wages are often low and inconsistent. They rarely get recognition for their hard work. Long hours take a toll on their health. Despite challenges, they keep building the city. They dream of a better life for their families. Their effort raises Chennai’s skyline, brick by brick.
